My Role

The Origin: From Campus Chaos to an Intelligent Platform

Working within the Rutgers Public Safety CSO Division, I had a frontline view of a systemic problem: operational chaos fueled by a fragmented ecosystem of manual tools. Shift scheduling lived in volatile Google Sheets, critical communications were lost in informal GroupMe chats, and repetitive questions from new hires consumed countless supervisor hours. This disjointed process led to costly errors and significant administrative overhead. I saw this not as a localized issue, but as a university-wide challenge. I conceived and built this platform from the ground up, not just as a fix, but as a scalable, AI-augmented, enterprise-grade solution to unify and automate operations for any department at Rutgers.

Platform Core Capabilities

AI Onboarding Assistant

A Vertex AI-powered chatbot serves as the first point of contact for new staff, answering SOP questions using both documents and live platform data.

Intelligent Scheduling & Conflict Engine

Real-time conflict checks against work-hour limits, existing assignments, and leave status prevent invalid shift swaps or requests automatically.

Supervisor Mandate Engine

Empowers supervisors to forcibly assign staff to open shifts with built-in guardrails to ensure compliance with all scheduling rules.

Granular Role-Based Access (RBAC)

Customized dashboards and permissions for every role, ensuring users only see and do what's relevant to them.

Automated Leave Management

Streamlined digital workflows for leave requests. Upon approval, the system automatically re-opens assigned shifts for coverage.

Comprehensive Audit Trails

Every critical action is logged in an immutable audit trail, ensuring full accountability and reporting integrity.

Technical Architecture: A Full-Stack Deep Dive

AI Assistant: Hybrid RAG with Vertex AI

The platform's standout feature is a multi-tenant chatbot powered by **Google Vertex AI**. I implemented a hybrid Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) approach. For **unstructured data**, each department has a dedicated Vertex AI Search datastore, indexed with their specific SOPs, policies, and training PDFs from Google Cloud Storage. For **structured data**, I used **Vertex AI Functions** to make secure, real-time API calls to our Node.js backend. This allows the chatbot to answer questions like, *"What are the procedures for event security?"* by searching documents, and also answer personalized questions like, *"Am I scheduled to work this weekend?"* by querying the live database.

Frontend: Responsive & Performant

The user interface is a dynamic single-page application built with **React.js**. I leveraged **React Context** for efficient and predictable global state management (handling user authentication, notifications, and role-specific UI). The styling is achieved via a custom, utility-first CSS approach for a clean, maintainable, and highly responsive design.

Backend: Robust & Scalable

A comprehensive suite of RESTful APIs, built on **Node.js** with the **Express.js** framework, powers the entire platform. Authentication is handled with secure **JSON Web Tokens (JWT)**, and the architecture is primed for a seamless future integration with Rutgers NetID SSO.

Database: Engineered for Integrity

The backbone of the application is a meticulously designed **MySQL** relational database. As shown in the complex ER diagram, the schema is highly normalized to ensure data integrity, prevent anomalies, and enable powerful, efficient querying. It's built not just for current needs but to scale with the university's demands.

The Vision: A Unified Operations Hub for Rutgers

This project was never just about solving one department's scheduling problem. It was a proof-of-concept for a university-wide transformation. The successful pilot led to a formal proposal to Rutgers leadership, outlining a phased roadmap to extend the platform to the Business School and beyond. The vision is to create a single, centralized "Rutgers Operations Hub" that handles academic scheduling, faculty assignments, resource booking, and student worker management—eliminating redundant software and saving the university an estimated **$100,000+ annually** in licensing fees while providing a tool perfectly tailored to its unique needs.
